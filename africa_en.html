Working in Africa: Lessons from Nigeria
October 2025

When I first came to Nigeria in 1997, I believed I was prepared. I had studied the federal system, reviewed industry reports, and analyzed economic structure. Yet, I soon discovered that the country on paper and the country on the ground were two completely different realities.

Nigeria is built on overlapping layers of ethnicity, regional leadership, lineage authority, and traditional power. Formal government decisions in Abuja are only one of many decision-making mechanisms. Real influence is distributed among governors, councils of elders, and traditional rulers whose authority predates the state itself.

Why the most patient, not the strongest, succeed in Nigeria

Western companies learned how to balance federal power, regional influence, and traditional authority. Many Russian projects failed because they assumed that signing an agreement in Abuja meant the work was complete. In Nigeria, that is only the beginning.

The core lesson

In Africa, business is not conducted through pressure or force. It is built through presence, relationships, and returning consistently over time.

You do not work in Africa. You work with Africa.
