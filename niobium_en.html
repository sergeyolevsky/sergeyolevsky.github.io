The Strategic Role of Niobium and Tantalum in Global High-Tech Value Chains
November 2025

Niobium (Nb) and tantalum (Ta) are metals essential to the resilience of critical high-tech industries: aviation, turbine energy systems, microelectronics, defense manufacturing and superconducting applications. Their physical properties ‚Äî high heat resistance, corrosion stability and the ability to form ultra-strong alloys ‚Äî make them indispensable where reliability and lifecycle matter.

Market concentration

Over 80% of global niobium supply comes from a single country ‚Äî Brazil. Tantalum resources are concentrated mainly in Central Africa and Australia. This creates supply chain dependencies shaped by logistical, regulatory and geopolitical conditions.

Jos Plateau, Nigeria

The Jos Plateau has been a center of columbite and tin extraction since the early 20th century. The resource base is matched by a complex system of land tenure and regional authority structures. In such regions, geology and institutional context are equally decisive.

From concentrate to strategic input

Value is created not at the mining stage, but during chemical separation, refining and alloy production. Control over processing hubs determines access to end-markets, not the raw ore itself.

Demand dynamics

Growth in high-strength steel production, turbine systems, semiconductor manufacturing and quantum technologies is driving sustained demand for Nb‚ÄìTa, with projected growth rates of 6‚Äì10% annually through 2035.

Conditions for stable integration

For regions like Jos to participate sustainably in global value chains, clarity of licensing, logistics stability, transparent corporate structures and balance between federal, regional and traditional authorities are critical.

These are the environments where the question of technological resilience for the next decade is being decided.
