Is There Gold in Nigeria and Can You Mine It? Yes. But Should You?
April 2026

Here is why.

So-called alluvial deposits of the open type are developed in roughly the same way: slurry, then gravity separation equipment such as jigs or sluices, followed by concentration. In simple terms, tin, columbite, tantalite, or gold can all be extracted using very similar technologies. The issue is not the technology. The issue is the grade — the amount of valuable concentrate per cubic meter of material.

There is gold in Nigeria — in Sokoto, Nasarawa, Zamfara, Minna, and beyond. However, some of these regions are influenced by groups such as Boko Haram. As a result, the usual problem of petty theft is compounded by political and religious risks. And that is a completely different level of exposure.

To operate in gold under such conditions, you need to address serious security issues. You must have your own people on the ground — essentially a private security structure. But this creates another risk. After Wagner, Africa has developed a very sensitive reaction to that kind of presence. If you bring in several dozen men with a certain profile who do not exactly look like geologists or processing specialists, local security services will almost certainly take an interest.

And in Nigeria, attention from security services always translates into additional costs. Or, as it is often called locally, additional PR.

Therefore, if you have your own tens of millions of dollars, it is more rational to build a mine and move directly into processing — for example, into ferroniobium production. If, however, you are operating in the top league — at the level of companies like Polyus or Norilsk Nickel — then you may consider going after gold.
