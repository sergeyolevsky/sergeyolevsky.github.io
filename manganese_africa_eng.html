Manganese. Africa: The Klondike Many in Moscow Failed to See
June 2026

I have always thought of Africa as a Klondike that many Russian representatives failed to notice.

My late father’s experience — and a critical mistake in 1994 — is what first led me to Nigeria in 1997.

He often said Nigeria had extraordinary natural wealth: gold, tantalum, zinc — everything literally under your feet.

In 1994, he was a major early-cooperative-era businessman facing pressure from competitors and creditors, with urgent financial gaps to close.

Through a contact — an officer — whose son claimed to be involved in “fast and profitable opportunities” in Nigeria, he entered a scheme that led to the disappearance of 680,000 dollars, stolen by Nigerian fraudsters.

In June 1994, my father and that officer’s son traveled to Nigeria for two weeks to try to recover the money.

The funds were never returned. One of those involved — Francis Shanu — was later arrested.

I have been connected to this region for nearly 30 years.

When colleagues asked me to find manganese in Africa, I first looked at South Africa — but everything was already locked under offtake agreements.

Through Nigerian contacts, I discovered that manganese also exists in Nigeria.

Formally, it barely appears in export statistics, but in practice it exists — and most of the flow is absorbed by Chinese buyers.

China’s presence in Africa became obvious in the early 2000s. In Nigeria today, it is no longer just trade — it is a parallel economic structure.

Chinese actors are present in mining, oil, banking, infrastructure, railways, and airports. In some areas, even informal competition exists with local structures.

When discussing manganese, Nigerian partners told me it follows the same pattern as lithium — early accumulation of control until the market is effectively closed.

There is still potential to build a serious resource base there, but it requires operational presence and understanding of local realities.

There is still no clear strategic understanding of Nigeria in Moscow, despite decades of attention.
